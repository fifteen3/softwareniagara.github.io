Our Mission

Our mission is to make Niagara a great place to live and work for designers, developers, and entrepreneurs.

We believe that each one of us has the power to make this mission a reality. Here's what we are doing to make it happen:

  • Organizing DemoCamp Niagara to showcase "Made in Niagara" technology
  • Organizing hackathons with a focus on open data and civic engagement
  • Operating #softwareniagara on IRC
  • Promoting Coworking and Startup Drinks
  • Promoting local startup and technology groups and events
  • Encouraging children and adults to develop computer literacy and programming skills

Want to help?

Everyone is welcome to participate in planning and organizing Software Niagara events. Connect with us on our issue tracker on GitHub. This is where we plan everything.

Coworking

Work independently in a social environment

Software Niagara hosts a weekly coworking session. Local designers, developers, and entrepreneurs gather to work independently on their software-related projects and ventures in a social and collaborative environment.

Coworking is a great way to escape the distractions of your home office while simultaneously satisfy your need for human companionship.

On Monday evenings

Join us every Monday night, except the third Monday of the month, at 7PM at Mahtay Cafe for coworking.

DemoCamp

Made-in-Niagara technology

Software Niagara holds quarterly DemoCamps to showcase made-in-Niagara technology and the local talent behind it all.

Each DemoCamp starts with a keynote, followed by up to six technology demos from local designers, developers, and entrepreneurs. The community has an opportunity to see new products and services, and ask questions of their developers.

DemoCamps have been held all over Canada, including Hamilton, Guelph, Toronto, and Calgary. We are proud to bring the tradition to Niagara. Everyone with an interest in technology is encouraged to attend.

DevTricks

Share & learn with your peers

Software Niagara hosts DevTricks on the third Monday of every month. DevTricks is a casual meetup where anyone can teach others about a new software technology, or computer setup, or programming language, and so on. It's an informal knowledge sharing event.

At each event, there will be a few scheduled speakers sharing their tips and tricks. After that, attendees are encouraged to get up and share their own tips and tricks. There will be a projector and laptop available. Finally, we will end the evening with a round table discussion to plan for what we would like to see at future DevTricks and to find volunteers to speak at the next event.

On the third Monday of the month

Join us on the third Monday of the month at 7PM at Mahtay Cafe for coworking. Everyone is welcome to learn and share.

Startup Drinks

Talk shop & have fun

Startup Drinks is an evening for sharing drinks (alcoholic and non-alcoholic) with your peers in the community and talking about startups. It's easy to participate.

  1. Grab a drink
  2. Find someone in the room
  3. Talk about startups

Startup Drinks is co-organized by Software Niagara and Coworking Niagara. There are no presentations or sponsors.

On the last Wednesday of the month

Startup drinks is held at 7PM on the last Wednesday of the month at Mahtay Cafe.

About Software Niagara

Software Niagara is a grassroots initiative to build a stronger software community and startup culture in the Niagara Region.

We are a group of local designers, developers, entrepreneurs, and students who believe that Niagara is a great place to create new technology and grow businesses. We believe that sharing, collaboration, and community spirit are vital to building a strong technology sector in Niagara.

We are trying to make Niagara a great place to live and work for designers, developers, and entrepreneurs by:

  • Organizing DemoCamp Niagara to showcase "Made in Niagara" technology
  • Organizing hackathons with a focus on open data and civic engagement
  • Operating #softwareniagara on IRC
  • Promoting Coworking and Startup Drinks
  • Promoting local startup and technology groups and events
  • Encouraging children and adults to develop computer literacy and programming skills

History

We started in 2012 with the goal of organizing technology and startup events to build a community that fostered innovation and excellence in Niagara. The inspiration for our grassroots effort came from Software Hamilton and Startup North.

Structure

Software Niagara is a grassroots group. All work is done by volunteers.

Contribute to Software Niagara

Ask a question, start a discussion, or contribute

Ask a question or start a discussion by creating an issue on GitHub. Watch our feedback repository to follow our activities. We use issues and milestones to plan events and activities. Please contribute if you can help us close an issue or hit a milestone.

Read past discussions

Check out closed issues to browse previous discussions. This is a great way to get a feel for our community.

Connect with us on Twitter

If you have a question or want to tell us something, ping us on Twitter at @softwareniagara. 140 character limits make everyone more efficient.

Join us on IRC

You can find local developers hanging out on our IRC channel. We are #softwareniagara on Freenode. Chat with us.

Email us

We're sorry it had to come to this, but if you cannot connect with us via any of the above channels, please do send an email to info@softwareniagara.com. Please remember, the quickest and most effective way to communicate with us is to connect with us on GitHub or Twitter. In other words, we may be slow to respond by email.

Contribute to this website

This website and its content are open source which means everyone is welcome to help us make it better. Furthermore, our blog is written by the community for the community. Please consider forking the website repository and submitting your blog posts in a pull request.

Please feel free to use our blog to:

  • Announce new apps, games, or services that you have built
  • Talk about interesting projects you are involved with
  • Share stories about starting your own business
  • Share tips and tricks of interest to designers, developers, and entrepreneurs
  • Announce tech- or startup-oriented events in or near Niagara
  • Introduce local user groups and meetups
